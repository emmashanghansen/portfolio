Case study

How we improved Nikita Hair's appointment booking flow

Nikita Hair saw high drop-off rates in their online bookings. As a UIX designer at Designhjelpen, I led a team of less experienced peers, aiming to accelerate their learning, grow my own skills and provide users with a smooth experience.

Client

Nikita Hair

My contribution

User research, Interaction design, Experience design, Project management

Team

Emma Shang Hansen (me), Brage Berg, Anne Selseng, Nanna Gleditsch, Sofie Røstum, Malin Iversen

Introduction

Bad UX → high user drop-off

Booking a hairdresser appointment should be quick and easy. Instead, users of Nikita's online booking found the experience confusing and the website difficult to navigate, with outdated visuals. Over 90% quit before completion, according to a third party analysis.

Nikita Hair used a complete salon- and booking system service to manage appointments and payment in all 200+ locations.

Prices were not included in the database. Each salon managed their own treatment lists, creating inconsistencies across the brand. This was not ideal. The system was rigid, greatly limiting how Nikita could improve the user flow.

The overarching challenge my team faced in this project, was technical limitations presented by this underlying system
